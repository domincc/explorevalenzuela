History of valenzuela

Valenzuela City’s history dates back to the pre-colonial era, following the city’s evolution from the rural province Polo, Bulacan to the vibrant city that we know today. On September 11, 1963, President Diosdado Macapagal signed Executive Order No. 46, forming the municipality of Valenzuela, named after its most famous son–Dr. Pio Valenzuela. “If not for Dr. Pio Valenzuela, there would be no Katipunan,” Valenzuela was the Surgeon General of the Katipunan, and he was essential in organizing many of the group’s movements and chapters, especially in Bulacan.

Explore Valenzuela

Valenzuela City is a thriving, highly urbanized city within Metro Manila, known for its excellent governance, robust business environment, and strong focus on community development

Arts in the City

A program for teens and children alike. Arts in the City is an arts workshop for music, drawing, painting, acting, dancing and other similar hobbies for those who want to learn and socialize with others. This program also serves as a culminating activity that showcases just how creative and artistic the youth of Valenzuela City are.

Museo ni Dr. Pio Valenzuela

A museum that depicts Dr. Pio Valenzuela’s life story through the various memorabilia displayed within its walls. The museum is built on the same site of his residence through the reimagination based on the recollections of his grandson, Arch. Arturo Valenzuela, allowing visitors to relive the experiences of the man whom Valenzuela City was named.

Valenzuela City Academic Center for Excellence (ValACE)

Located at McArthur Highway cor., A. Pablo St., the Valenzuela City Academic Center for Excellence (ValACE) is a result of the collaboration between the Valenzuela City Hall and the Office of Senator Sherwin “Win” Gatchalian. It has 3 stories for a public library, 1 floor for the Education 360 Program Office, and 2 full floors for teacher training and seminar rooms.

Bread & Flower Festival

Valenzuela City celebrates the Bread & Flower Festival every 12th of November, in honor of their patron saint, San Diego de Alcala. While dancing in the streets, dancers featuring both children and adults wear colorful costumes decorated with bread and flowers. This festival was based on a legend, where San Diego de Alcala used to smuggle bread out of the convent to give to the poor, until one day flowers spilled from his robe instead.

Museo Valenzuela 

After its renovation in 2017, Museo Valenzuela is currently a historical and cultural center of Valenzuela that displays the works and life of the city’s namesake. It is iconic for its vintage look that is visited by thousands of people and as an event center where Arts in the City sessions are held.

Bell Tower of San Diego de Alcala Church

Found in Barangay Poblacion, the belfry, or the bell tower of San Diego de Alcala Church serves as a cultural and religious relic of the bygone Spanish era. Along with the entrance arc, the tower is the only remaining structure of the original church built in 1632.

Arkong Bato Park

This park is adjacent to the historic stone arch in barangay Arkong Bato, with a linear size of about 3,500 square meters. This park includes green open space with sitting areas, a fountain, and a monument of one of our heroes: Captain Delfin Velilla.

Fatima Avenue

Located along Mc Arthur Highway, this pedestrianized avenue serves as a green corridor connecting the National Shrine of Our Lady of Fatima and the Museo Valenzuela. Fatima Avenue also has a wide array of outdoor dining establishments while being a popular jogging circuit.

Allied Local Emergency Response Teams (ALERT) Center - Egbert Canon

The cannon passed by many pedestrians serves as a memorial for Colonel Harry C. Egbert and Filipino Soldiers who fought in the Battle of Malinta in 1899. As the story goes, Filipino soldiers were defending Malinta
